Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a wall that covers much of the northern border of China. The length of all the portions of the wall built by every Chinese Dynasty, plus various branches, the total comes to 13,171 miles long. The Wall construction started by the Qin Dynasty and following dynasties continued to work on it. Later the Ming Dynasty rebuilt the wall.

Chichén Itzá El Castillo (Mexico)

Chichen Itza means "at the mouth of the well of the Itza". Located on the Yucatan Peninsula, Chichen Itza was a large city built by the Maya people. It thrived from about 600 AD., until 1221, when the power in the region shifted to Mayapan.

Petra (Jordan)

Inhabited since prehistoric times and situated between the Red Sea and the Dead Sea. Petra is half-built, half-carved into the rock, and is surrounded by mountains riddled with passages and gorges. It is one of the world's most famous archaeological sites, where ancient Eastern traditions blend with Hellenistic architecture.

Machu Picchu (Peru)

Machu Picchu is the site of ancient Inca ruins. It is perched above the Urubamba River valley in a narrow saddle between two sharp peaks—Machu Picchu (“Old Peak”) and Huayna Picchu (“New Peak”)—at an elevation of 2,350 metres. It's one of the few major pre-Columbian ruins found nearly intact.

Christ The Redeemer (Brazil)

Colossal statue of Jesus Christ at the summit of Mount Corcovado. The statue was completed in 1931 and stands 30 metres tall, its horizontally outstretched arms spanning 28 metres. The statue has become emblematic of both the city of Rio de Janeiro and the whole nation of Brazil.

Colosseum (Italy)

The Colosseum is the largest amphitheater (meaning “theatre in the round”) in the world! Oval in shape, it measures 189m long, 156m wide and 50m high (about the height of a 12 storey building).

Taj Mahal (India)

An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife. The Taj Mahal is the jewel of India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.
